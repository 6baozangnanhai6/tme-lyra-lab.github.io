QinYu: A High Fidelity Zero-Shot TTS with High Naturalness, Spontaneous Colloquialism and Emotional Control

QinYu Team
TME Lyra Lab

Abstract

We introduce QinYu, a high-fidelity text-to-speech system engineered to deliver speech with exceptional naturalness, 32kHz studio-quality audio, spontaneous colloquialism for conversational scenarios like podcasts, and fine-grained emotional control for narrative contexts like audiobooks. QinYu excels in generating natural paralinguistic elements such as authentic laughter and precise prosodic pauses, surpassing the 22kHz limit of most open-source TTS systems. Its scenario-specific variants include QinYuCast, which automates insertion of colloquial artifacts like pauses and hesitations for spontaneous dialogue, and QinYuInstruct, which enables emotion specification via natural language descriptions like "warm" or "excited". Future iterations will advance an "ALL-in-One" architecture built on a million-hour-scale base model, integrating controllable paralinguistic tagging, adjustable colloquialism strength, large-model enhancements, and novel voice generation capabilities, bridging the gap between synthetic and human speech across diverse TTS applications.
